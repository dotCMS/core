# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences share this surface:

- **Travelers** browsing for inspiration: people deciding where to go next, reading
  destination stories and blog posts, searching for places and experiences. They
  arrive in a discovery mood, not a transactional one, often on mobile, often
  scanning before committing to read.
- **dotCMS evaluators**: developers, content teams, and prospects who land on this
  reference example to judge what a dotCMS + Next.js front end can look and feel
  like. The home page is a portfolio piece that has to read as a real, shippable
  premium brand, not a scaffold.

## Product Purpose

TravelLux is the demo brand for the dotCMS + Next.js headless example. The home
page is the flagship surface: it presents destinations, blog stories, and search,
all driven by dotCMS content and editable in the Universal Visual Editor (UVE).
Success is a page that (1) makes a traveler want to keep scrolling and (2) makes a
dotCMS evaluator think "I want our site to feel like this" — while staying fully
content-managed and UVE-editable.

## Brand Personality

Elevated, editorial, and inviting. The voice is that of a well-traveled friend with
taste: confident, specific, never shouty. Premium without being cold; warm without
being a discount pitch. Photography leads, type supports, color is used with intent.
Three words: **considered, spacious, vivid**.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / AI-template look.** No cream body background, no tiny uppercase
  tracked eyebrow above every section, no identical icon-card grids, no gradient
  text, no hero-metric template. If it could be mistaken for an AI-generated SaaS
  landing page, it has failed.
- **Cold corporate / fintech.** No navy-and-gray, stiff, impersonal enterprise feel.
  Travel must feel human and inviting.
- **Loud discount-travel OTAs (Expedia, Kayak).** No deal-badge clutter, no
  price-screaming density. This is premium discovery, not bargain hunting.
- **The saturated editorial-typographic lane.** "Editorial" here means spacious,
  photography-forward, confident hierarchy — NOT the Klim/Fraunces magazine
  fingerprint (display-serif-italic + small mono labels + ruled three-column grid).
  Carry the editorial feel through imagery, scale, and restraint, not that template.

## Design Principles

1. **Let the photograph be the design.** Destination imagery is the hero of every
   fold. Type and chrome frame it; they don't compete with it.
2. **Confident restraint.** One committed brand color and a clear type hierarchy do
   more than a busy palette. Whitespace is a feature, not empty space.
3. **Content-managed by construction.** Every striking moment must survive being
   driven by dotCMS fields and edited in UVE. No effect that only works with
   hardcoded content.
4. **Specific over generic.** Real place names, real stories, real photographs.
   No filler copy, no decorative stand-ins for imagery.
5. **Editorial feel, not editorial template.** Evoke a premium travel publication
   through pacing and photography, while deliberately sidestepping the saturated
   serif-italic-magazine aesthetic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 against its background (including text over imagery,
which needs scrims/overlays to hold contrast); large text ≥3:1. Visible
`:focus-visible` states, full keyboard paths (including the AI search dialog),
semantic landmarks and headings. Every animation has a
`prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` alternative. The page must remain legible and
navigable when content length varies (long/short titles, missing images).
